Music

I'm always listening to music: seeking new artists out and trying to see live music whenever it comes to Denver or Boulder. Growing up I played a few different instruments, but the only one that I've stuck with to any degree is the guitar. The best place to find out about what I'm listening to is my Last.fm profile, which records tracks that have been played from many software media players or even devices like iPhones and iPods. I get a good amount of music recommendations from my friends, but I also keep up with several sources online:

  • Pitchfork has been an "indie" tastemaker for a while now, and I still find most of their reviews to at least be well written, even if I don't agree with all of their ratings.
  • Stereogum is kind of the template for MP3 blog/zines. They don't have formal reviews like Pitchfork, but there's always a constant stream of news and new tracks.
  • The ISO50 blog is a joint effort between Scott Hansen/Tycho and several other authors. There's always a wide and eclectic selection of music streaming here, from 4-track selections to original albums.
  • The Black Cab Sessions is a unique venue for a performance: the back seat of a moving cab. It forces the artists to adapt to the space and it often brings out a different side to well-known songs.

Craft Beer

Just like any food or drink, there are good beers and there are not-so-good beers, and there is often an inverse correlation between the size of the brewery and the taste of its beer. This is difficult not to notice in Colorado with the ready supply of breweries and craft beer-related events, like the Great American Beer Festival.

I've never home-brewed myself, but I do frequent a few local brew pubs:

  • Avery Brewing
  • Boulder Beer Company
  • Mountain Sun & Southern Sun
  • Oskar Blues
  • Twisted Pine
  • Walnut Brewery
